Prevent further damage and loss of data:

  1. Disconnect from the internet:

    As soon as you suspect an attack, disconnect your PC from the internet to prevent the ransomware from spreading to other devices on your network or communicating with its command and control servers.

  2. Determine the type of ransomware:

    Identify the type of ransomware that has infected your PC. This can help you determine what type of ransomware you are dealing with and what tools and resources you can use to try to recover your files.

  3. Back up your files:

    If possible, back up your important files to an external hard drive or a cloud-based storage service. This can help you restore your data if you are unable to decrypt your files or if you decide to wipe your PC and reinstall your operating system.

  4. Scan your PC with antivirus software:

    Use reputable antivirus software to scan your PC for malware, including the ransomware. If the antivirus software detects the ransomware, follow its instructions to remove it.

  5. Check for available decryption tools:

    Some security companies and law enforcement agencies have released decryption tools that can help you recover your files without paying the ransom. Check their websites for information and instructions.
    It depends on the specific ransomware variant. Some decryption tools are free and others may require payment or the help of a professional IT security expert. Some of decryption tools I had used:

    1. Avast Decryption Tools:

      Avast provides free decryption tools for some ransomware families, including AES_NI, Alcatraz Locker, Apocalypse, BadBlock, Bart, BTCWare, Crypt888, CrySiS, Globe, HiddenTear, Jigsaw, LambdaLocker, Legion, NoobCrypt, SZFLocker, and many more.

    2. Emsisoft Decryptor:

      Emsisoft offers free decryption tools for some ransomware families, including Amnesia, Aurora, Damage, HydraCrypt, Marlboro, Nemucod, Stampado, and more.

    3. Kaspersky RakhniDecryptor:

      Kaspersky offers a free decryption tool for the Rakhni ransomware family.

    4. TrendMicro Ransomware File Decryptor:

      TrendMicro provides free decryption tools for several ransomware families, including CryptXXX, Crysis, Jigsaw, and TeslaCrypt.

    It is important to note that not all ransomware can be decrypted, and even if a decryption tool is available, it may not work for all cases. Some ransomware variants use strong encryption that cannot be broken, while others have flaws in their encryption that can be exploited to recover the files.

  6. Professional help and guidelines:

    If you are unable to recover your files on your own, consider seeking the help of a professional IT security expert or a specialized ransomware recovery firm. Report the attack to law enforcement and other relevant authorities. This can help them track down the attackers and prevent further attacks.
    There are several reasons why paying the ransom is not a good idea. First, there is no guarantee that the hackers will actually provide the decryption key to unlock your files. Even if they do, there is no guarantee that the key will work or that they won't demand more money.
    Second, paying the ransom encourages the hackers to continue their criminal activities and target other people. By paying, you are providing them with the financial means to carry out further attacks.
    Third, there is always a risk that the hackers will use the information you provide during the payment process to steal your identity or carry out further attacks
